About Thyroid Disease


What is Thyroid Disease?

Thyroid is a gland present near the windpipe (trachea). It releases hormones such as T3 and T4, which control multiple vital functions of the body, such as metabolism, body temperature. The dysfunction of the thyroid gland and improper release of the T3 and T4 hormones causes thyroid disease.The pituitary gland handles this malfunction by releasing the Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH).There are two types of thyroid conditions. Hypothyroidism is a condition wherein TSH is released in insufficient amounts by the thyroid gland. Hyperthyroidism is a condition wherein TSH is produced in excess amounts by the thyroid gland.


What causes Thyroid Disease?

Anyone can get Thyroid Disease. Some people are more at risk than others. Some things that increase your risk for thyroid disease include:

  • Have a family history
  • Have medical condition like
    • Pernicious anemia
    • Diabetes
    • Primary adrenal insufficiency
    • Rheumatoid arthritis
    • Sjögren’s syndrome
    • Turner syndrome
  • Take a medication that’s high in iodine
  • Being over 60 years old, especially in women.
  • Have had treatment for a past thyroid condition or cancer

What are the symptoms of Thyroid Disease?

You may notice one or more of the following symptoms when thyroid dysfunction occurs:

Symptoms of an overactive thyroid (hyperthyroidism) can include:

  • Losing weight
  • Anxiety, irritability and nervousness
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Having an enlarged thyroid gland or a goiter
  • Feeling sensitive to heat
  • Muscle weakness and tremors
  • Irregular menstrual periods

Symptoms of an underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism) can include:

  • Gaining weight.
  • Experiencing forgetfulness.
  • Dry and coarse hair
  • Intolerance to cold temperatures
  • Feeling tired
  • Frequent and heavy menstrual periods.

What are the complications that arise due to Thyroid disease?

Throid gland help your whole body work properly. When you have Thyroid diseas, you can also have problems with how the rest of your body is working. Some of the common complications of Throid disease include Eye problems, bone disease, heart disease, swollen skin, nerve damage, thyrotoxic crisis and myxedema .

What are the types of Thyroid disease?

  • Overt hyperthyroidism
  • Overt hypothyroidism
  • Subclinical hyperthyroidism
  • Subclinical hypothyroidism

How do I know if I have Thyroid disease?

Thyroid usually does not have very differentiable symptoms until it turns into goitre or myxedema. The only way to know how well your thyroid gland is working is to get tested.
